# Military Research Sources Guide

**Purpose:** Guide for conducting authoritative military research for simulation parameter tuning, scenario design, and authenticity verification.

---

## Authoritative Military Sources

### Jane's Defense Publications

**Primary sources for:**
- Weapon systems specifications
- Platform capabilities
- Sensor systems
- Military technology

**Access:**
- Jane's Fighting Ships (naval platforms)
- Jane's All the World's Aircraft (air platforms)
- Jane's Weapon Systems (weapons and sensors)
- Jane's Defence Weekly (current developments)

**Credibility:** HIGH — Industry standard for defense information

---

### Official Military Technical Manuals

**Primary sources for:**
- Operational procedures and doctrine
- Technical specifications and capabilities
- System integration details
- Maintenance and training

**Access:**
- U.S. Army Field Manuals (FMs)
- U.S. Navy Technical Manuals (TMs)
- NATO publications
- Other national military publications (many are publicly available)

**Credibility:** HIGH — Official sources, highest authority

---

### Defense Industry Publications

**Primary sources for:**
- Manufacturer specifications
- System performance data
- Technical characteristics
- Development programs

**Access:**
- Manufacturer white papers and specifications
- Industry publications (Aviation Week, Defense News)
- Press releases and product announcements
- Trade show presentations

**Credibility:** HIGH — Direct from manufacturers, but may be marketing-oriented

---

### Government Defense Databases

**Primary sources for:**
- Official defense statistics
- Procurement information
- Budget and program data
- Historical records

**Access:**
- U.S. Defense Department publications
- Government Accountability Office (GAO) reports
- Congressional Research Service reports
- Other national defense databases (where publicly available)

**Credibility:** HIGH — Official government sources

---

### Academic Military Research

**Primary sources for:**
- Historical analysis and lessons learned
- Tactical doctrine evolution
- Strategic studies
- Comparative analysis

**Access:**
- Military academies and war colleges (US Army War College, Naval War College)
- Defense universities and research centers
- Academic journals (Journal of Strategic Studies, Military Review)
- Think tank publications (RAND, CSIS, Brookings)

**Credibility:** HIGH — Peer-reviewed or editorial oversight

## Source Evaluation Criteria

### Authority and Credibility

**HIGH Credibility:**
- Official military publications
- Jane's Defense Publications
- Academic peer-reviewed research
- Industry standards and specifications

**MEDIUM Credibility:**
- Industry analysis and market reports
- Defense industry publications
- Academic technical reports
- Open-source simulation projects

**LOW Credibility:**
- Player community feedback
- Enthusiast forums
- Blogs and opinions
- Social media discussions

---

### Publication Date and Relevance

**Recent (within 3 years):** Highest relevance for current capabilities and trends
**Historical (3-10 years):** Good for established systems and conventions
**Archive (10+ years):** Valuable for historical analysis, but may be outdated for current capabilities

---

### Cross-Reference Validation

**Best Practices:**
- Find multiple independent sources for critical data
- Prioritize primary sources over secondary sources
- Note discrepancies between sources
- Investigate conflicts rather than assuming one source is correct
- Use source consensus as validation

---

## Research Quality Assurance

### Documentation Standards

**Always Include:**
- Source citations with URLs
- Publication dates and version information
- Confidence levels in gathered data (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW)
- Notes on discrepancies between sources
- Recommendations for primary sources when available

**Source Credibility Notes:**
- Authority and expertise of source
- Potential biases or limitations
- Currency and relevance
- Cross-reference validation

---

### Research Methodology

**Step 1: Identify Research Needs**
- What information is needed?
- What level of detail is required?
- What sources are most appropriate?
- What are the time constraints?

**Step 2: Search Authoritative Sources**
- Start with primary sources (military publications, Jane's)
- Cross-reference with secondary sources
- Note discrepancies and conflicts
- Document all sources consulted

**Step 3: Evaluate Source Credibility**
- Assess authority and expertise
- Check publication date and relevance
- Verify cross-reference validation
- Note biases or limitations

**Step 4: Format and Document**
- Structure findings clearly
- Cite all sources with URLs
- Note confidence levels
- Highlight discrepancies
- Provide context for use

## Common Research Scenarios

### Weapon System Parameters

**Sources:**
- Jane's Weapon Systems
- Manufacturer specifications
- Military technical manuals
- Defense industry publications

**Parameters to research:**
- Range and accuracy
- Warhead type and yield
- Guidance system
- Propulsion system
- Launch platforms

---

### Platform Capabilities

**Sources:**
- Jane's Fighting Ships / All the World's Aircraft
- Official military specifications
- Manufacturer data
- Open-source intelligence

**Parameters to research:**
- Performance (speed, range, endurance)
- Operational capabilities (payload, sensors, weapons)
- Technical characteristics (dimensions, displacement, power plant)
- Service history and variants

---

### Sensor Systems

**Sources:**
- Jane's Radar and Electronic Warfare Systems
- Manufacturer specifications
- Technical manuals
- Academic research

**Parameters to research:**
- Range and resolution
- Frequency band and power
- Detection probability
- False alarm rate
- Countermeasure effectiveness

---

### Tactical Doctrine

**Sources:**
- Military field manuals and doctrine publications
- Historical operations analysis
- Academic military research
- Veterans' accounts

**Parameters to research:**
- Command and control procedures
- Tactical formations and maneuvers
- Rules of engagement
- Communication protocols
- Decision-making processes

---

### Historical Operations

**Sources:**
- Official military histories
- After-action reports
- Declassified documents
- Veteran memoirs and oral histories

**Parameters to research:**
- Timeline and participants
- Tactical decisions and outcomes
- Technical factors
- Lessons learned

## Conclusion

Use this guide to conduct authoritative military research for simulation development. Prioritize primary sources, cross-reference multiple sources for validation, document all sources with confidence levels, and note discrepancies between sources. Research quality directly impacts simulation authenticity and player trust.
